Echeveria 'Lola' — Translucent Pearlescent Rosette

Echeveria 'Lola' is a hybrid prized for its perfectly symmetrical, translucent pink-lavender rosette — a collector staple that holds its neat form with minimal grooming. SouthQuest Farm grows comparable pearlescent Echeveria hybrids in Yunnan, China.

About This Cultivar

'Lola' is a hybrid between E. lilacina and E. tippy, combining lilacina's soft pastel colouring with a tighter, more symmetrical rosette form. Its translucent, slightly waxy leaves shift between pale lavender and dusty pink depending on light exposure.

Care Basics

  • Light: Bright, direct-to-indirect light; holds its form well across a range of conditions
  • Water: Soak-and-dry; relatively slow-growing, so less frequent watering than fast-offsetting Echeveria
  • Propagation: Leaf-pull and offsets, though slower to establish than many cultivars
  • Common issue: Minimal — Lola is considered one of the more forgiving named Echeveria cultivars
