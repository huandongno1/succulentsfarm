Echeveria Agavoides — The Agave-Like Echeveria

Echeveria agavoides is named for its agave-like, thick pointed leaves arranged in a tight star-shaped rosette — a distinctive form among Echeveria species. SouthQuest Farm's Echeveria catalogue focuses on Korean-line variegated cultivars; contact us for sourcing advice on agavoides-type forms.

About the Species

Echeveria agavoides is native to central Mexico and grows as a stemless, symmetrical rosette of thick, firm, triangular leaves that taper to a defined point — visually closer to a small agave than a typical soft-leaved Echeveria. Popular cultivars include 'Lipstick' (bright green with scarlet red tips) and 'Ebony' (near-black).

Care Basics

  • Light: Bright, direct sun for strongest red tip colouration
  • Water: Soak-and-dry; the thick leaves store more water reserve than thinner-leaved species
  • Propagation: Leaf-pull or offsets; slower-growing than many Echeveria
  • Common issue: Loss of red tip colour in low light — this is reversible with brighter conditions
