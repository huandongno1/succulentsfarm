Classic Cultivar Guide

Echeveria 'Perle von Nürnberg' — The Gateway Succulent

Echeveria 'Perle von Nürnberg' is a classic hybrid cultivar prized for its dusty mauve-pink, powder-coated rosettes — often the first named Echeveria new collectors buy. SouthQuest Farm grows closely related purple-toned Echeveria hybrids in Yunnan, China.

About This Cultivar

'Perle von Nürnberg' was bred in Germany in the early 20th century, one of the earliest named Echeveria hybrids and still among the most widely propagated today. Its powdery mauve-pink colouring and reliable, easy-going growth habit have made it a mainstay of the succulent trade for decades.

Care Basics

  • Light: Bright, direct-to-indirect light for at least 6 hours daily to maintain colour
  • Water: Soak-and-dry; avoid wetting the rosette
  • Propagation: Leaf-pull and offsets both work well
  • Common issue: Loss of the powdery bloom from handling — this doesn't regenerate on the affected leaf

Perle von Nürnberg — what buyers ask

Echeveria 'Perle von Nürnberg' is a classic hybrid cultivar known for its dusty mauve-pink rosettes with a powdery bloom finish. Often called a gateway succulent for new collectors, it's one of the most widely sold Echeveria cultivars in Western retail.
Our closest match is 'Purple Joy', a purple-toned variegated cultivar from our Korean-line breeding program, though the colour pattern differs from the solid dusty-mauve of true Perle von Nürnberg. Share a reference photo and we'll confirm the closest match in stock.
The waxy, powdery coating (epicuticular wax) on many Echeveria is easily rubbed off by handling and does not regenerate on that leaf. Handle rosettes by the base rather than touching the leaf surfaces to preserve the bloom.
