Echeveria Lilacina — The Ghost Echeveria

Echeveria lilacina, known as Ghost Echeveria, is prized for its broad, powdery lilac-grey leaves in a loose open rosette — a common parent species behind many pastel-toned hybrid cultivars. SouthQuest Farm grows comparable pearlescent Echeveria hybrids in Yunnan, China.

About the Species

Echeveria lilacina is native to Oaxaca, Mexico, forming a low, open rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves coated in a soft powdery bloom that gives it a ghostly lilac-grey appearance. It's widely used in hybridising programs for its pastel colouring and compact growth habit.

Care Basics

  • Light: Bright, direct-to-indirect light; tightest rosette form and strongest colour develop in full sun
  • Water: Soak-and-dry; drought-tolerant once established
  • Propagation: Leaf-pull works reliably
  • Common issue: Loose, stretched rosette in low light — move to a brighter position
