Aeonium Canariense — The Giant Dinner-Plate Rosette

Aeonium canariense produces one of the genus's largest single rosettes — up to 50–80cm across, growing near-stemless rather than branching into multiple heads. SouthQuest Farm's largest single-form specimens come from our own old-stalk breeding lines in Yunnan, China.

About the Species

Aeonium canariense is a Canary Islands endemic that puts all its growth into a single, dramatic rosette rather than branching — unlike arboreum, which forms multiple heads on a woody stalk. Mature specimens can reach 50–80cm in diameter, making it one of the largest-rosette species in the genus.

Care Basics

  • Light: Bright, indirect light; can tolerate more shade than smaller Aeonium species
  • Water: Soak-and-dry; larger rosettes hold more moisture reserve than smaller forms
  • Propagation: Slower than branching species — typically grown from seed or occasional basal offsets rather than stem cuttings
  • Common issue: Rosettes can flatten and split under their own weight if not given adequate support as they mature
